Borscht (also spelled borshch) is a traditional Ukrainian soup that's hearty, flavorful, and perfect for cold days. Here's a recipe for Ukrainian Borscht:

Ingredients:

2 large beets, peeled and grated
2 medium carrots, peeled and grated
1 large onion, finely chopped
2 cloves garlic, minced
2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
4 cups beef or vegetable broth
1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
1 small head cabbage, shredded
1 bay leaf
2 tablespoons tomato paste
2 tablespoons vegetable oil
Salt and pepper, to taste
1 tablespoon white vinegar
Sour cream, for serving
Fresh dill, chopped, for garnish
Instructions:

Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add chopped onions and minced garlic. Saut√© until onions are translucent, about 5 minutes.

Add grated beets and carrots to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are tender, about 10 minutes.

Pour in the beef or vegetable broth and add diced potatoes, shredded cabbage, diced tomatoes (with their juices), bay leaf, and tomato paste. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Simmer uncovered until the vegetables are tender, about 20-25 minutes.

Stir in white vinegar. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.

Remove the bay leaf from the soup before serving.

Serve hot, garnished with a dollop of sour cream and chopped fresh dill.

Enjoy your hearty Ukrainian Borscht!
